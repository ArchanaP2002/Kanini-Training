ODOR


Odor is an important characteristic of food, influencing food choice behavior. It is well known that odors can be appetizers (e.g. smell of freshly baked bread). Short exposure to food odors enhance salivation and hunger feelings. On the other hand, people report to be less hungry after cooking a meal (long exposure).





TASTE


The taste of food is caused by its chemical compounds. These compounds interact with sensory (receptor) cells in your taste buds. The cells send information to your brain, which helps you identify the taste. Humans can recognize several types of tastes





NUTRITION


Nutrient-rich (or nutrient-dense) foods are low in sugar, sodium, starches, and bad fats. They contain a lot of vitamins and minerals and few calories. Your body needs vitamins and minerals, known as micronutrients. They nourish your body and help keep you healthy
